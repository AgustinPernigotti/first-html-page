Google releases predictions of popular holiday gifts from Apple iPhone, PlayStation 5 to Fat Brain Simpl Dimpl

Kelly Tyko
USA TODAY

Published: 5:30 a.m. ET Nov. 12, 2020 | Updated: 9:11 a.m. ET Nov. 12, 2020
Google predicts 7 gifts to expect this holiday season
These are a few gifts that Google predicts your loved ones will want to find under the tree on Christmas morning. USA today

Before you make a list and check it twice, Google has a tip for you: These are the 100 holiday gifts it predicts your loved ones will want to find under the tree on Christmas morning.

Many of the products on the Google Shopping 100 list, which the tech giant shared exclusively with USA TODAY, will come as no surprise with the latest Apple products – Watch Series 6, iPhone 12 and iPad Air 4 – new Sony PlayStation 5 and Microsoft Xbox Series X included . Three Google products including two versions of the Pixel and Google Chromecast with Google TV also made the list.

The list, posted at https://shopping.google.com, was compiled by combing through several months of Google search data, the company said, identifying which products had risen in popularity and extrapolating to make a "forward-looking prediction of popular products."

Many of the products on the Google Shopping 100 list will come as no surprise with the latest Apple and Google products and gaming systems.

“This is only our prediction of what’s going to trend, so it will be interesting to see how things actually play out over the shopping season,” Google spokesperson Craig Ewer told USA TODAY. “If there’s anything we’ve learned this year, it’s that 2020 is full of surprises!”

Holiday sales have kicked off earlier than past years with more deals being offered online-only as retailers look to reduce crowds and eliminate the need to rush into a store.

Walmart unveiled last week how it will revamp its Black Friday sale by offering shoppers three chances to get the “best prices of the season” in November.

Scott McCall, Walmart U.S. executive vice president and chief merchandising officer, said in a recent interview with USA TODAY that more online offers makes for a "safer and more convenient way to shop" and helps manage in-store traffic.

"The doorbuster deals will be online, but we're also going to have great prices in-store,” McCall said.
